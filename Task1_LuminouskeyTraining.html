Black Iris (Iris nigricans)

Appearance: Deep blackish-purple petals, approximately 12 cm in diameter.
Habitat: Endemic to Jordan; commonly found near Madaba.
Blooming Season: Spring.
Significance: National flower of Jordan; endangered due to habitat loss.
Features: Requires direct sunlight and well-drained soil.



Golden Drop (Podonosma orientalis)

Golden Drop (Podonosma orientalis) is a perennial flowering plant in the Boraginaceae family, native to the Eastern Mediterranean and parts of Western and Southern Iran. In Jordan, it thrives in regions such as Wadi Zerka Main and the sandstone gorges of Petra.
Botanical Description

Growth Form: A low-lying shrub (chamaephyte) with stems and leaves densely covered in green or whitish hairs.
Flowers: Cylindrical corolla-shaped flowers ranging in color from yellow to white and light blue, blooming between February and June. Wikipedia
Foliage: The plant sheds its leaves between August and October.
Habitat and Distribution

Golden Drop is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, commonly found on vertical and sloping hard rock outcrops, especially limestone formations in garrigue terrains. In Jordan, it is prevalent in areas like Wadi Zerka Main and the sandstone gorges of Petra. Its range extends across the Eastern Mediterranean, including Syria, Lebanon, Israel, Iraq, and parts of Iran. Orchidroots
Traditional Uses

The plant contains saponins, natural compounds that produce a soapy lather. Traditionally, locals have used its leaves and stems to clean cookware and polish metalware. In folk medicine, a mixture of macerated leaves and wheat flour was applied as a heated compress to alleviate migraine headaches. Additionally, its flowers are considered edible.
Cultural Significance

In Arabic, the plant is known as "maṣīṣ" (مصيص), a name also adopted in Hebrew. Its presence in various Middle Eastern cultures underscores its ecological and cultural importance.
